ChatGPT Project Proposal

Problem Description

ChatGPT is a Large Language Model (LLM) developed by OpenAI that is capable of completing a wide range of natural language tasks, such as text generation, translation, and summarization. It is also able to generate code and document it well. All of this can be done with prompts given by the user. It is trained on text data scraped from the internet, which includes 25% of Reddit posts and various academic articles and books.

With how fast technology is advancing, Yejin Choi, an associate professor at the University of Washington and her colleagues have been researching ways algorithms can remove potentially offensive words and phrases from training data. Others have tried to manually remove bias but it only results in hurting marginalized communities, which include Black people, Muslims, and people who identify as LGBTQ+ are particularly disadvantaged. Their research aims to exhibit weakness in these models so they can better understand what aspects can be improved. However, there are many other disadvantages to biased models. One of the main concerns is the potential bias in the data used to train the model, as well as the implications of data collection from social media platforms such as Twitter and Reddit, where users have no way to opt out. Additionally, there are discussions about the potential misuse of ChatGPT, such as in academic misconduct and plagiarism. To address these concerns, we propose to start healthy discussions among researchers, students, and educators about the positive and negative outcomes of ChatGPT, and to explore ways to leverage its capabilities while avoiding its potential harms.

We as a team want to help people understand:

  • What is ChatGPT in the first place?
  • What can you actually use it for?
  • What are the pros and cons of the uses?
  • What are the implications of ChatGPT for our infrastructure? Our lives?

Proposed Application

As mentioned earlier, we want to help people understand ChatGPT and how we can use it. Most of our users will be tech enthusiasts, university students, market researchers, and generally, people who want to learn more about the power of ChatGPT and use it to their advantage. However, we want to make our application welcoming for people who are usually not interested in news about AI so that they can learn how technological advancement is changing their lives whether they know it or not. Doing so, we want to spread awareness of how powerful ChatGPT is and how harmful it can be.

  • A page with tweets about ChatGPT that users can sort by date or filter by the type of tweet. By being able to read other peoples opinions, we hope that the user will develop their own opinons based on the information provided.
